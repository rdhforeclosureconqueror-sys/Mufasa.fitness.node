TITLE:
Phase 9 – Adaptive Training Intelligence (Deterministic)

STATUS:
Complete. The deterministic adaptation engine, derived member history, member insights, dashboard projections, protections, and regression coverage are implemented. No deployment or push was performed.

TRAINING HISTORY:
A reusable summary now derives completed weeks, completed and missed sessions, adherence percentage and history, progression/deload/maintain/health-review history, incomplete weeks, exercise completion percentage, and average sleep, soreness, and energy from existing persisted member records. It does not persist or mutate the summary and supports legacy generated-plan records.

ADAPTATION ENGINE:
A versioned, deterministic rule engine evaluates long-term history and returns a future-facing recommendation only. It can recommend following an assigned program, completing health review, extending recovery, reducing progression rate, reviewing progression earlier, or maintaining the current level.

RULES:
Assigned-program precedence and health-review restrictions are evaluated first. Two recent deloads recommend recovery emphasis; multiple recent incomplete weeks reduce the progression rate; at least three completed weeks and 90% adherence recommend earlier progression review. Recommendations never skip a phase, identify at most one variable for review, never select exercises, and do not modify equipment, plans, executions, or historical records. No AI, machine learning, or randomness is used.

MEMBER INSIGHTS:
Deterministic, non-diagnostic statements report session adherence, excellent recovery when persisted thresholds qualify, and the applicable maintain, recovery, progression-review, assigned-program, or health-review recommendation. No medical advice or clearance is provided.

DASHBOARD:
The existing progress dashboard now exposes a derived consistency score, adherence trend, progression trend, recommended focus, next milestone, and member-readable insights. The authenticated /api/me/training-adaptation endpoint exposes the complete derived model.
